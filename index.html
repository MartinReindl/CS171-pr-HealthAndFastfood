Fast Food and Health

This is an interactive visualization that allows you to explore the relationship between fast food restaurant locations and measures of poor health. Use the radio controls below to change the health measures displayed on the map and scatterplot. Each state in the map is colored based on the percentage of adults diagnosed with the selected health measure. The dots correspond to the geographic locations of individual restaurants (color coded by restaurant name). The scatterplot shows the relationship between number of restaurants per million (x-axis) and the percentage of adults diagnosed with the selected health measure (y-axis). Each point in the scatterplot corresponds to a state. The line represents a linear regression in an attempt to more explicitly show the relationship. Use the checkboxes below to filter the restaurants to be displayed on the map and to be factored into the number of restaurants per million in the scatterplot. Click on states on the map to update the selections shown in the bar charts to see more detailed information about that state.
